Health Insurance Claims Process Guide
Northstar Health Plan - schema-aligned synthetic workflow
1. Purpose and data relationships
This guide describes how a claims pipeline can receive a claim, validate its schema, join it to the plan reference table, assign a workflow status, and preserve an auditable result. It is designed for extraction, RAG, and workflow-testing exercises.
Canonical join: claims.plan_id -> plans.plan_id
2. Plan reference used during adjudication
Important: the source labels are synthetic. The dataset happens to list a higher deductible for Gold PPO than for the HMO plans; the guide preserves those values rather than replacing them with typical market assumptions.
3. Standard claims workflow
1. Receive and log: Capture claim_id, member_id, plan_id, procedure, claim_amount, status, and date_filed.
2. Validate schema: Confirm required fields are present; validate date format, numeric amount, unique claim_id, and permitted status values.
3. Validate plan linkage: Join the claim to plans.csv by plan_id. Claims with an unknown plan_id should be rejected or pended for correction.
4. Enrich claim context: Attach plan_name, annual_deductible, copay_pct, coverage_type, and network_tier to the processing record.
5. Review duplicate risk: Check whether the same member, procedure, amount, and filing date already exist under another claim_id.
6. Apply configured rules: Use only implemented benefit, authorization, network, and pricing rules. These rules are not supplied by the current CSV schema and must not be invented.
7. Assign outcome: Use Approved, Denied, or Pending as represented in the supplied data. Store a reason field in a production schema even though claims.csv does not contain one.
8. Produce audit output: Preserve original values, enriched plan values, processing timestamps, rule results, and any manual override details.

5. Validation controls
- claim_id must be nonblank and unique.
- member_id and plan_id must be nonblank.
- plan_id must exist in plans.csv.
- claim_amount must be numeric and nonnegative.
- date_filed must parse as a valid date.
- status must be one of Approved, Denied, or Pending unless the schema is deliberately expanded.
- Do not infer coverage from procedure name or status alone.
6. Supplied synthetic claim register
The plan cost-share percentage is shown only as reference data. The file does not provide enough information to calculate member responsibility, because deductible accumulation, allowed amount, service rules, and payment details are absent.

9. Data dictionary
plans.plan_id: Unique plan identifier and join key.
plans.plan_name: Human-readable plan name.
plans.monthly_premium: Monthly premium amount.
plans.annual_deductible: Annual deductible amount.
plans.copay_pct: Percentage field used as the plan cost-share reference.
plans.coverage_type: Plan type category: PPO or HMO in this dataset.
plans.network_tier: Synthetic tier label: Gold, Silver, or Bronze.
claims.claim_id: Unique synthetic claim identifier.
claims.member_id: Synthetic member identifier.
claims.procedure: Procedure label supplied for the claim.
claims.claim_amount: Claim charge amount supplied in the record.
claims.status: Approved, Denied, or Pending.
claims.date_filed: Date the claim was filed.
